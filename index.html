What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that starts in the breast tissue. It is the most common cancer among women worldwide, and can also occur in men.

Statistics on Breast Cancer Cases

According to the American Cancer Society, in 2021:

  • An estimated 281,550 new cases of invasive breast cancer will be diagnosed in women in the US.
  • 41,760 women will die from breast cancer in the US.
  • About 1 in 8 women in the US will develop invasive breast cancer during their lifetime.

Classification into Malignant and Benign

Breast cancer can be classified into two types: malignant and benign.

  • Malignant breast cancer means that the cancerous cells can invade nearby tissues and spread to other parts of the body.
  • Benign breast cancer means that the cancerous cells do not invade nearby tissues or spread to other parts of the body.

Summary

Breast cancer is a serious disease that affects many women worldwide. By understanding the statistics and classifications of breast cancer, we can work towards developing effective methods for prevention, early detection, and treatment.
